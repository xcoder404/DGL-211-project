SIMON SAYS...

Welcome, I am Simon Crespo, a nature, wildlife, and travel photographer, and I would love to share my story with you. I have been fortunate to embark on a remarkable journey through the natural world, spanning the entire Pacific Northwest and beyond, for over 25 years.

In this time, I've had the privilege of having my work featured in esteemed publications such as National Geographic, Canadian Geographic, and the Canadian Tourism Commission. I've also had the pleasure of collaborating with respected brands like Fairmont Hotels, Subaru, Perception Kayaks, and Readers Digest, who have entrusted me with the task of visually conveying their stories.

My photographic adventure began back in 1995, on the captivating landscapes of northern Vancouver Island. Over the years, my appreciation for the natural beauty of this region has expanded my horizons to encompass the vast landscapes of the entire Pacific Northwest and beyond. I've been incredibly fortunate to bear witness to the splendor of these places, and I've endeavored to share their beauty through my lens.

One facet of this journey that has left me truly humbled is my deep connection with orcas. This bond led me to contribute to documentaries and film projects focused on these magnificent creatures. Over seven years, I dedicated myself to capturing unique footage, showcasing orca whales as they surf gracefully behind larger vessels. It's an accomplishment that fills me with both pride and humility.

Beyond my collection of fine art prints that have found their place in homes and offices, I am equally dedicated to passing on the knowledge and passion that have driven my journey. I offer workshops, webinars, and private lessons to help others on their own photography adventures, with the hope of inspiring and nurturing the talents of emerging photographers.

In the realm of nature and wildlife photography, I consider myself a humble participant, honored to share the beauty of the Pacific Northwest and beyond. My work is not just about the photos; it's a reminder of the importance of cherishing and conserving the wonders of our planet.

With gratitude and respect,

Simon Crespo
